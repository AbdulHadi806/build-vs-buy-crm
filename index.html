Key Decision Factors

Factors Favoring BUYING

Time-to-Market

Salesforce can be configured and deployed in weeks. Crucial if you're under pressure to scale quickly.

Reliability and Security

Battle-tested infrastructure, compliance (SOC 2, GDPR), and automatic updates.

Integration Ecosystem

Out-of-the-box connectors to tools like Slack, Gmail, Stripe, QuickBooks, etc.

Support and SLA

Access to support, documentation, and enterprise uptime guarantees.

Cost Predictability

Subscription model makes budgeting easy; fewer surprises than with custom dev.

Non-technical teams

Easier for sales/marketing/support to use without relying on engineers.

Factors Favoring BUILDING

Unique Business Logic

If your workflows or sales process is very domain-specific, a custom build allows perfect fit.

Long-term Cost Savings

Over 3–5 years, avoiding per-user license fees can outweigh initial build costs.

Flexibility & Control

Full control over UI/UX, data structure, and roadmap—no vendor lock-in.

Internal Dev Resources

If you have a skilled internal team with bandwidth, you reduce marginal cost.

Integration Control

Custom APIs or niche integrations can be tailored more precisely.

Conclusion

Unless your CRM needs are extremely unique and justify a long-term investment with full internal control, buying Salesforce is far more cost-effective and faster in terms of ROI—especially in Year 1.

Building internally becomes viable only if:

You already have idle developer capacity
You need deep customization not possible in Salesforce
You want to avoid per-user licensing in the long run (break-even in 3–4 years)
